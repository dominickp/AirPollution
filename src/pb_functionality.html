Overview Data Functionality Design Implementation Evaluation

Functionality

Tasks

  • Filter The initial view presented to the reader will show pollution data filtered for their city of choice.
  • Navigate After the reader sees information about his city, the visualization zooms out to show information about the whole country. Additionally, when the user clicks on a country, they will be presented with the underlying city level detail.
  • Select The reader will be able select additional countries to add to the visualization. This will provide the flexibility for the user to choose which country comparisons are most relevant or interesting.
  • Sort The bar chart with the countries can be sorted based on PM2.5 or PM10.
  • Determine Range The visual encoding we have chosen uses bars to represent the range of pollution for each country. We think this is an important metric and therefore it is presented using the very effective position encoding.
  • Coordinate The city that is picked when you enter the page is the main focus of all visualizations. It is the city that will be explored in the beginning, the country that will be used to compare to well known polluters and the city that will be the main focus on the map.
  • Coordinate TIn the section “Behind the numbers: factors contributing to pollution” readers will be able to explore coordinated scatter plots showing PM2.5 vs 9 different metrics such as % fossil fuel use, % forestry in country. We also plan to include functionality for simultaneous highlighting and filtering.
