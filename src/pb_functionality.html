Functionality

Tasks

  • Filter The initial view presented to the reader will show pollution data filtered for their city of choice.
  • Navigate After the reader sees information about his city, the visualization zooms out to show information about the whole country. Additionally, when the user clicks on a country, they will be presented with the underlying city level detail.
  • Select The reader will be able select additional countries to add to the visualization. This will provide the flexibility for the user to choose which country comparisons are most relevant or interesting.
  • Sort The bar chart with the countries can be sorted based on PM2.5 or PM10.
  • Determine Range The visual encoding we have chosen uses bars to represent the range of pollution for each country. We think this is an important metric and therefore it is presented using the very effective position encoding.
  • Coordinate The city that is picked when you enter the page is the main focus of all visualizations. It is the city that will be explored in the beginning, the country that will be used to compare to well known polluters and the city that will be the main focus on the map.
  • Coordinate TIn the section “Behind the numbers: factors contributing to pollution” readers will be able to explore coordinated scatter plots showing PM2.5 vs 9 different metrics such as % fossil fuel use, % forestry in country. We also plan to include functionality for simultaneous highlighting and filtering.

Must Have Features

Act 1: Air

  • Selectable city with a text input using Typeahead.js
  • Allow the user to put in their home city to filter the data to their city.
  • Show PM2.5 value of selected city on a scale from 0-300(?)
  • Show a line with the limit of the WHO Safe level
  • Show PM2.5 value of well known places like New Delhi as comparison
  • Switch between PM2.5 and PM10 value.
  • Auto-populated commentary based on city selection
  • Display facts based on the city date, like “twice as much pollution than the average cities in the US”.
  • Add and remove additional cities also using Typeahead.js - This will give the viewer the ability to compare their city data to others

A year in the life of Beijing’s air.

  • One year time series of Beijing PM2.5 data with interactive narration.
  • Chart will be presented in a multi-step chronological sequence, stopping at each key event to explain to the user the main points. This will provide a tacit tutorial while giving the reader the ability to move forward and backwards to the key events.

Behind the numbers: factors contributing to pollution

  • Small multiples charts showing PM2.5 scatter plot correlations with 6 key metrics
  • Coordinated views with highlight ability.

Act 2: Death

  • Show different causes of mortality in a chart (well known causes like aids or malaria). - Animate the chart with human-icons (Inspiration).
  • Zoom in to show how air pollution consists of deaths of different diseases.
  • Show percentages
  • A legend to show what a skeleton means
